Mass Surveillance, Digital ID, and What You Can Do

A practical privacy guide: what is tracked, what isn’t, and concrete steps to reduce exposure.

← Back to Blog Install DedSec Project

1) What “mass surveillance” usually means

In practice, surveillance is often data collection at scale: location signals, device identifiers, app analytics, ad IDs, and network metadata. The goal is often profiling and prediction, not a movie-style live feed of your life.

2) Digital ID: convenience vs centralization

Digital ID can reduce paperwork and fraud, but it can also centralize identity proof. The key questions are: what data is stored, who can access it, what logs exist, and what independent oversight and appeals you have.

3) Your threat model (the step people skip)

Start by defining what you’re protecting and from whom: advertisers, data brokers, stalkers, or criminals. Different threats require different tools; otherwise you waste effort on the wrong defenses.

  • Low effort, big gain: updates + strong passwords + 2FA
  • Higher effort: app minimization, permissions, compartmentalization

4) Concrete steps on Android

Turn off permissions you don’t need (location/mic/camera), reset your advertising ID, disable always-on background access for noisy apps, and keep OS + apps updated. This reduces passive tracking dramatically.

5) The “privacy stack” that is worth it

Use a password manager, enable 2FA, and prefer end-to-end encrypted messaging when possible. Add a reputable VPN only when it solves a real problem (public Wi‑Fi, ISP visibility), not as a magic invisibility cloak.
