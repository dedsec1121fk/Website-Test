4) The checklist (do this first) — how to explain it to a friend (Media / Forensics)

Checklist for Media Forensics: Checking Images and Video (Fast). Don’t try to do it all today — pick 3 items, finish them, then come back.

  • Update devices and enable automatic updates.
  • Revoke old sessions/tokens after suspicious events.
  • Enable login alerts and review them weekly.
  • Minimize the data you store and share.
  • Encrypt your device and lock screen.
  • Use a password manager and unique passwords.
  • Review app permissions and remove unused apps.
  • Back up important data with version history.

  • Turn on MFA for your most valuable accounts.

8) Quick FAQ — signals that you’re improving (Media / Forensics)

Quick FAQ for Media Forensics: Checking Images and Video (Fast). These answers are written to be practical, not perfect.

Where should I start if I’m overwhelmed?

Pick one account, one device, one backup. Finish those, then expand. Progress beats perfection.

How often should I review access and permissions?

Monthly for key accounts, quarterly for everything else, and immediately after staffing/vendor changes.

What should I write down during an incident?

Time, symptoms, what you checked, what you changed, and what still isn’t explained. Keep it short and factual.

How can teams apply this without slowing down?

Make the secure path default: templates, checklists, least‑privilege roles, and small automated checks.

How do I talk about this without scaring people?

Use scenarios and habits: “Here’s what it looks like, here’s what we do, here’s why it helps.”

What’s a good “end” to a long post like this?

A repeatable plan: one change to ship, one drill to run, one metric to watch for 30 days.

What’s the difference between being safe and being resilient?

Safe reduces likelihood; resilient reduces damage and recovery time when something still goes wrong.

Do I need expensive tools for this?

No. Most wins come from settings, process, backups, and a short drill you actually run.

How do I know if a message is “urgent” or just trying to rush me?

Assume urgency is a tactic. Pause, verify via a second channel, and check in the official app/site.

What is the single fastest improvement I can make today?

Turn on MFA where it protects money or identity first, then revoke old sessions and reduce permissions.

Teams fail at the seams: shared access, unclear ownership, and ‘temporary’ exceptions.

  • Assign owners: every system has a human responsible for access and recovery.

  • Write a one‑page incident checklist: who does what in the first hour.

  • Keep an inventory of critical vendors, renewals, and key contacts.
